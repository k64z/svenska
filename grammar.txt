## SUBJUNCTIVE WITH "OM" CLAUSES
Rule: Present tense for real conditions, past forms for hypothetical
Ex: Om det regnar, stannar jag hemma (real). Om jag vore rik, skulle jag resa (hypothetical)
Notes: "vore" (were), "hade" (had) for unreal situations

## PRESENT PARTICIPLE
Rule: infinitive + -nde
Ex: springande, kommande
Notes: ongoing action, often translated to "-ing" in English

## PAST PARTICIPLE
Rule: group 1: -ad, group 2: -d, group 3: -en
Ex: köpt -> köpad, bott -> bodd, skriva -> skriven
Notes: describes completed actions, used in passive constructions

## PASSIVE VOICE WITH -S
Rule: Many verbs form passive by adding -s to the infinitive
Ex: kalla -> kallas (is called), se -> ses/syns (is seen/appears)
Notes: Common alternative to "bli/vara" + past participle

## IMPERATIVE MOOD
Rule: Remove -a ending from infinitive for most verbs
Ex: köpa -> köp!, stanna -> stanna!, sälja -> sälj!
Notes: Used for commands, advice, instructions

## DEFINITE ARTICLES
Rule: -en words -> -en, -ett words -> -et, plural -> -na-/-en
Ex: hus -> huset, bil -> bilen, bilar -> bilarna
Notes: some irregulars (man -> mannen, fot -> foten)

## WORD ORDER (V2)
Rule: verb always 2nd position in main clauses
Ex: Igår åkte jag hem. Jag åkte hem igår

## PRESENT PERFECT
Rule: har + supine (-t/-it/-tt ending)
Ex: har ätit, har gjort, har bott
Notes: supine != past participle

## ADJECTIVE AGREEMENT
Rule [indefinite]: en-words +∅, ett-words +-t, plural +-a
Rule [definite]: always + -a
Ex: en stor bil, ett stort hus, stora bilar, den stora bilen

## POSSESSIVES
Rule: min/mitt/mina, din/ditt/dina, sin/sitt/sina (reflexive)
Ex: min bil, mitt hus, mina böcker
Notes: sin/sitt/sina = subject owns, not someone else

## PAST TENSE GROUPS
Rule: Group 1: + -ade, Group 2: + -de, Group 3: vowel change 
Ex: prata -> pratade, bo -> bodde, komma -> kom
Notes: Group 4 irregulars exist (gick, åt)

## MODAL VERBS
Rule: modal + infinitive (no att)
Ex: kan simma, vill äta, ska åka
Notes: position like English modals

## REFLEXIVE VERBS
Rule: verb + sig/mig/dig/oss/er depending on subject
Ex: jag tvättar mig, han rakar sig, vi träffas
Note: some verbs always reflexive (heter, känss)

## TIME EXPRESSIONS
Rule: tillbringa tid = spend time, spendera pengar = spend money
Ex: tillbringa helgen hemma, spendera mycket på mat

## CONDITIONAL MOOD
Rule: skulle/borde + infinitive (hypothetical situations)
Ex: Jag skulle köpa det om jag hade pengar
Notes: skulle = would, borde = should/ought to

## CONDITIONAL & SUBJUNCTIVE
Rule: skulle + infinitive (would), vore (were), present for real conditions
Ex: Om jag vore rik, skulle jag resa. Om det regnar, stannar vi hemma
Notes: "hade" + supine for past conditional (Om jag hade vetat...)

## "DET SER UT ATT" CONSTRUCTION
Rule: det ser ut + att + infinitive (predictions about future)
Ex: Det ser ut att regna, det ser ut att bli kallt
Notes: "ser ut" = looks/appears, often used with "bli" for future states
